Event Summary
On July 20, AEI’s Shane Tews hosted an expert panel discussion on the vital function spectrum serves to consumers, businesses, and US national security. The panel featured the Center for Strategic and International Studies’ Clete Johnson, Rysavy Research’s Peter Rysavy, the Brattle Group’s Paroma Sanyal, and the Digital Progress Institute’s Joel Thayer.
Mr. Rysavy highlighted 5G’s unique capacities, including its ability to operate at multiple frequencies, its reliability, and its function as a springboard for economic activity. Dr. Sanyal echoed the revolutionary capabilities of spectrum, arguing there is no substitute.
Mr. Johnson disused the national security impact of falling behind in spectrum, explaining that China’s growing spectrum leadership opens the door to swaths of data in the hands of an authoritarian police state. If the US loses its position, China could dominate communications infrastructure, cyber operations, and weapons systems.
Mr. Thayer led the closing conversation on the Federal Communication Commission’s spectrum auction authority, which was revoked in early March 2023. He spoke about this function’s vital role in quickly and efficiently delivering spectrum to the market. Mr. Rysavy drew parallels between spectrum’s security uses and its communication and economic uses, explaining that commercial strength is national security.
—Kate Beinkampen
Event Description
Join us at AEI for an enlightening discussion on the vital role of spectrum use in bolstering the American economy. Our panel of experts will delve into various key topics, including the restoration of the Federal Communication Commission’s auction authority, the significance of a robust 5G broadband infrastructure, and innovative technological advancements that can address current spectrum-sharing challenges. This is an opportunity to gain valuable insights and explore potential solutions for refilling the nation’s spectrum pipeline with full-power, licensed spectrum.
Hear from AEI’s Shane Tews, Clete Johnson of the Center for Strategic and International Studies, Peter Rysavy of Rysavy Research, Paroma Sanyal of Brattle Group, and Joel Thayer of Digital Progress Institute to gain valuable insights into the evolving landscape of spectrum management and its impact on the future of mobile broadband networks.
