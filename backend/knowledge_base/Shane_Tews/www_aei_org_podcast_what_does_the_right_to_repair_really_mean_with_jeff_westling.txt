	What Does the Right to Repair Really Mean? (with Jeff Westling) | American Enterprise Institute - AEI

Our digital devices, smart as they are, do occasionally need repair. What most consumers may not know is there is a difference between the service from a licensed repair shop and an unlicensed mall kiosk or quick “fix-it” stop at the strip mall. Original replacement parts for devices are important to keep them running and keep concerns about loss of data or security of the device at bay. The “right to repair” legislation we are seeing in state legislatures is strong on DIY repair to give individuals repair shop options, but there is a dark side to these shops to consider. What happens to the intellectual property of the device’s creators? How might third-party parts affect product security? What effects would this have on R&D and competition? To guide us through this debate, we are joined by
Jeff Westling
, the Director of Technology and Innovation Policy at the American Action Forum, as he draws from his recent
article
on the subject.
In this episode,
Shane
walks us through how a simple repair can become complicated and what both producers and consumers need to consider when investing and maintaining our digital assets as this debate grows.
