Event Summary
On November 17, AEI’s Shane Tews was joined by Federal Communications Commission (FCC) Commissioner Brendan Carr and former FCC Chairman Ajit Pai to discuss the Biden administration’s approach to spectrum policy and the upcoming World Radiocommunication Conference, which will set the agenda for global spectrum policy.
With the White House releasing their
national spectrum strategy
earlier this week, Commissioner Carr and Mr. Pai argued that the current administration’s approach to spectrum threatens to leave the US lagging behind China, with inefficiencies often characteristic of European spectrum slowly creeping into American infrastructure.
The panel also touched on the FCC’s new rules regarding digital discrimination released this week. While Commissioner Carr and Mr. Pai acknowledged the noble aims of such policy, they argued that the sweeping and vague nature of the rules could potentially hurt those same providers that will allow underserved and rural communities to receive broadband. The conversation concluded with questions from the audience, including a comparison of the FCC’s approach under Mr. Pai’s leadership to the actions of the current administration.
—Owen O’Brien-Powers
Event Description
Efficient spectrum management is vital to American leadership in wireless innovation. However, under the Biden administration, the Federal Communications Commission (FCC) has failed to enact a comprehensive spectrum strategy, with no spectrum auctions or spectrum bands in the FCC’s pipeline.
On November 17, AEI will host a panel discussion examining the current administration’s seemingly backward approach to spectrum management. Panelists will address the impact of 5G leadership and explore potential policy solutions to enable continued wireless innovation.
