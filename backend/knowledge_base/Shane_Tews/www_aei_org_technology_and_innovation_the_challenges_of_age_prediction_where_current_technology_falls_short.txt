	The Challenges of Age-Prediction: Where Current Technology Falls Short | American Enterprise Institute - AEI

In an era where digital identity verification is becoming increasingly important, age-prediction software has emerged as a potential solution for age-gating and access control. However, recent
evaluations
by the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) have shed light on the complexities and limitations of this technology.

Here are my key concerns surrounding the accuracy of age prediction and where the most significant inaccuracies occur:
No One-Size-Fits-All Solution
. One of the primary findings from NIST’s evaluation is that no single algorithm exists for age prediction. The accuracy of these algorithms varies significantly based on factors such as image quality, gender, region of birth, and the person’s age in the photograph. This variability poses a challenge for widespread implementation, as different algorithms may perform well for specific demographic groups while falling short for others.
Gender Disparities.
A concerning trend observed in the NIST evaluation is the consistently higher error rates for female faces than male faces. This gender-based disparity in accuracy has persisted since an earlier assessment in 2014, and the underlying reasons remain unknown. Such discrepancies raise essential questions about the fairness and reliability of age-prediction technology across genders.
Demographic Sensitivities
. The evaluation revealed that different algorithms have varying sensitivities to demographic groups. While an algorithm might perform admirably for one group, it could produce significantly less accurate results for another. These inconsistencies across demographics highlight the need for more robust and inclusive training data to improve accuracy.
Age-Related Inaccuracies.
The age of the individual being analyzed also affects the accuracy of predictions; certain age ranges appear to be more challenging for the software to estimate accurately. The lack of accuracy could lead to issues in applications where precise age verification is crucial, such as online safety measures for minors or age-restricted content access.
Ongoing Improvements and Limitations
. Despite these challenges, there is some good news. The accuracy of age-estimation software has improved over the past decade, with mean absolute errors decreasing from 4.3 to 3.1 years when estimating ages from a common database of visa photos. However, more than just an improved error rate may be required for applications requiring precise age verification.
Implications for Implementation.
The limitations in accuracy raise essential questions about the appropriate use of age-prediction software in various contexts. For applications where precise age verification is crucial, such as legal age restrictions or child protection measures, the current state of the technology may not be reliable enough. Organizations considering implementing age prediction systems must carefully weigh the potential benefits against the risks of inaccurate predictions.
NIST’s commitment to releasing updates every four to six weeks underscores the speed at which age estimation technology is evolving. This ongoing evaluation process is crucial for identifying areas for improvement and driving innovation in the field; however, it also highlights the technology’s limitations and the need for caution in its implementation.
As research and development in age-estimation software continue, we should expect to see further improvements in accuracy and reliability. Meanwhile, addressing the current limitations will require concerted efforts to diversify training data, refine algorithms to perform consistently across demographics, and develop more sophisticated evaluation methods.
Beyond the technical challenges, a 2007 paper by
Adam D. Thierer
discussed
the multifaceted issues surrounding age-verification requirements for social networking sites, highlighting the challenges and potential consequences surrounding age verification. Thierer highlighted several of the challenges and potential consequences involved with implementing age-verification mandates that still hold concerns today, such as privacy concerns and potential infringements on First Amendment rights.
The complexities and drawbacks of enforcing age verification requirements demand careful consideration. The recent NIST evaluation highlights the need to thoroughly assess the effectiveness of current technology for the task at hand while also weighing the implications for privacy, security, and practical implementation of age-gating measures. Moreover, the NIST report underscores the importance of rigorous testing and addressing demographic disparities to achieve more accurate and equitable age-estimation solutions.
As we navigate this evolving landscape, it’s crucial to remember that the legal rights of minors should not be overshadowed by enthusiasm for technological fixes that have yet to achieve the necessary accuracy levels for reliable age verification. Moving forward, it’s essential to strike a balance between technological innovation and the protection of individual rights, ensuring that any regulatory frameworks built around age-prediction technology are both effective and respectful of personal freedoms.
