	The Battle for Global Internet Governance (with Dominique Lazanski) | American Enterprise Institute - AEI

China claims it is tired of western-centric internet governance, and is now seeking to establish a less centralized global internet based on newer, native products that reflect China’s policy agenda. What would the success of China’s model mean for the future of internet governance? What would it mean for human rights?
On this episode,
Shane
is joined by
Dominique Lazanski
, principal consultant of Last Press Label, a UK-based consultancy that specializes in global cybersecurity and internet policy. They discuss Dominque’s new essay titled “
Standardising the Splinternet: How China’s Technical Standards Could Fragment the Internet
,” which looks at the relationship between China’s technological ambitions and the rise of a decentralized internet marketplace.
