Event Summary
On November 2, AEI gathered an expert panel to discuss the complex issues surrounding online speech and content moderation.
The discussion began with an overview of the legal issues affecting courts’ online speech decisions, including the First Amendment, Section 230, and the upcoming NetChoice cases. University of North Carolina’s Matt Perault struck an optimistic yet cautious tone, noting the dangers avoided thanks to existing policies while warning of pitfalls such as poorly crafted regulations.
AEI’s Michael Rosen touched on the relationship between social media platforms and the Israel-Hamas conflict, explaining that platforms have a duty to cover key stories, but they must establish appropriate safeguards to protect victims, children, and sensitive users.
The panel then turned to government mandates and jawboning. TechFreedom’s Ari Cohn argued that users displeased with platforms could simply leave, while NetChoice’s Steve DelBianco emphasized that the government has no legal footing to restrict a platform’s content.
The panel concluded with a conversation about the trade-offs in crafting policies that safeguard children without unduly limiting expression.
—Kate Beinkampen
Event Description
Online speech is a cornerstone of modern democracy, but it is also a complex and rapidly evolving landscape. Several high-profile court cases and legislative debates regarding online speech are before us, raising important questions about balancing freedom of expression among other important societal values.
On November 2, AEI will host a panel discussion exploring the legal, economic, and societal impacts of today’s online speech decisions. Panelists will discuss key topics of the latest legal and regulatory developments around online speech and the challenges of regulating online speech in a globalized world.
