	Is Success a Sequence? How Choices Affect Outcomes | American Enterprise Institute - AEI

The
success sequence
shows that people who finish high school, work full-time, and marry before having children have a low chance of experiencing poverty. Yet fostering and developing those behaviors is complex and affected by countless external factors.
In this episode, host
Brent Orrell
joins
Isabell Sawhill
of the Brookings Institution and
Ian Rowe
, a visiting fellow at AEI and Fordham Institute  and cohost of AEI’s “
Are You Kidding Me?
” podcast. Together, they discuss ways to help young people find and maintain pathways to success as they enter adulthood.
