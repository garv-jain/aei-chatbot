Event Summary
On February 14, AEI’s Brent Orrell was joined by Fiona Hill of the Brookings Institution, AEI’s Michael Strain, and AEI’s Kori Schake to discuss Dr. Hill’s book “There’s Nothing for You Here: Finding Opportunity in the 21st Century” (Mariner Books, 2021).
The panelists discussed similarities and differences in economic degradation and populist movements in the US, the UK, and Russia. Dr. Schake responded to Dr. Hill’s thesis, agreeing in part to the similarities among the three countries but noting cultural and structural differences that make the American situation unique. Dr. Strain engaged Dr. Hill’s economic argument, pointing to the resiliency of American dynamism and warning that a country’s biggest problem should not be taken as the national narrative.
The panel discussed policy solutions for the places in the United States that have been left behind economically, including how to invest in places versus people, public-private partnerships, and small business loans.
— Jessie Wall
Event Description
Do the social and economic conditions in the UK and US mirror those that fed Russian authoritarianism?
Fiona Hill’s memoir, “There Is Nothing for You Here: Finding Opportunity in the 21st Century” (Mariner Books, 2021), seeks to answer that question by telling the story of her upbringing in forgotten parts of England. Dr. Hill argues that while economic despair, lagging mobility, and political polarization were the predicates to Vladimir Putin’s rise, they also threaten the social and political stability of our own country. The solution, she says, depends on building new ladders of opportunity for isolated and disadvantaged populations here at home.
Join Dr. Hill as she discusses her book with AEI’s Kori Schake, Michael Strain, and Brent Orrell.
