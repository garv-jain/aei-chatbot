	Robert Doar Receives APHSA’s Lifetime Achievement Award | American Enterprise Institute - AEI

AEI’s incoming president, Robert Doar, was honored last week by the American Public Human Services Association with their Lifetime Achievement Award (see “Congratulations to Our 2019 Member Awards Recipients!” visible through
this link
).
APHSA, a bipartisan, nonprofit organization representing state and local human services agencies, selected Robert for this honor in recognition of his significant contributions to the well-being of children and families throughout his career designing, implementing, and managing human services programs for the state and city of New York. APHSA also highlighted Robert’s work as AEI’s Morgridge Fellow in Poverty Studies, which has established him as one of the nation’s leading thinkers on poverty issues.
Those of us who have worked closely with Robert know these achievements are driven by a passionate commitment to ensuring that poverty programs work as a springboard for long-term, employment- and family- focused economic and social well-being. His leadership has been crucial in helping AEI establish a
pragmatic anti-poverty approach
for policymakers charged with assisting Americans who seek economic mobility and the full enjoyment of our nation’s economic life. His distinguished list of
accomplishments and publications
includes his service as a co-chair of the
National Commission on Hunger
and his leadership of the bipartisan
AEI-Brookings Working Group on Poverty and Opportunity
, which generated consensus recommendations for improving opportunities for economic mobility among poor and working-class Americans.
On behalf of all of my AEI colleagues, I want to offer our hearty congratulations to Robert for this prestigious and well-deserved recognition and honor.
