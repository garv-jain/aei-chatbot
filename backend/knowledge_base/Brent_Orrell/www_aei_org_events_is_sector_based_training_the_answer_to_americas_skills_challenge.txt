Event Summary
On February 26, AEI’s Brent Orrell hosted an event on the effectiveness and research behind sector-based training strategies on workforce development.
After Mr. Orrell’s opening remarks, panelists Richard Hendra of MDRC, Kadeem Noray of Harvard University, and Garrett Warfield of Year Up discussed their respective research findings and perspectives. Dr. Hendra provided a broad understanding of sector-based training and its proven effectiveness. Dr. Warfield then went into the work and findings at Year Up, and Dr. Noray displayed research on Year Up’s programs and its impact on various communities and demographics. Following the panel discussion, the event concluded with a Q&A.
—Josh Boynton
Event Description
Evidence for “what works” in job training and workforce development remains remarkably thin. The exception to this rule is sector-based training, which has been found to substantially increase employment and wages, especially for workers who might otherwise find themselves trapped in low-skill, low-wage jobs.
Join AEI’s Brent Orrell and a panel of experts for a discussion exploring the keys to scaling targeted training programs effectively, including model replication, adaptable funding, and the integration of comprehensive support services such as childcare and transportation. Discover how these strategies can amplify the impact of sector-based training, creating a more dynamic and responsive workforce capable of adapting to rapidly evolving skill needs.
