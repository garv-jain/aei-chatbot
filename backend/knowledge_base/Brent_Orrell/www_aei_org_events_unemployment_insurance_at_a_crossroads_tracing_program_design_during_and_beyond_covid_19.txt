Event Summary
On October 27, AEI’s Brent Orrell, Mason M. Bishop, and Matt Weidinger and Amy Simon of Simon Advisory discussed unemployment insurance (UI) reform. The conversation was framed around Ms. Simon’s recent report, “Unemployment insurance at a crossroads: Tracing program design during and beyond COVID-19,” which explores how UI operated during the pandemic, the UI system before the pandemic, and how reforming the system should look going forward to ensure that it can handle crises.
Panelists discussed the sustainability of funding, coverage of persons including independent contractors, standardization across states, technological modernization, and federal-state partnerships that could increase program effectiveness. They considered balancing unemployment fraud with immediate crisis need and how to account for post-pandemic labor market dynamics.
— Jessie Wall
Event Description
COVID-19 unemployment insurance (UI) programs were developed and implemented as an emergency policy response, but as the pandemic progressed, the programs grew and changed. Amy Simon’s recent
AEI report
explores how policymakers should address systemic, operational problems in the UI system and make productive changes regarding fraud detection and prevention, federal-state partnerships, and system modernization.
Ms. Simon will offer remarks on her report, after which AEI’s Matt Weidinger and Mason M. Bishop will give comments preceding a panel discussion moderated by AEI’s Brent Orrell. The participants will address UI program design, system performance during the pandemic, and reform proposals regarding funding and incentive structures, program standardization, and covered populations.
