	Trailer: Introducing the National Criminal Justice Month Miniseries | American Enterprise Institute - AEI

Thirteen years ago, Congress designated
March as National Criminal Justice Month calling for public education on the causes
of crime, crime prevention, and the criminal justice system itself.
This month on
Hardly Working,
we are adopting this mission in spirit and action
through a series of podcasts looking at several key aspects of criminal justice
policy. The series will include interviews with senior researchers working to
understand crime and the criminal justice system; how to encourage desistance
from criminal behavior, reform of the way we prosecute and incarcerate
offenders, and we’ll also hear the stories of individuals who went to prison
and then turned their lives around.
It is our hope these conversations
lead listeners to ask more questions, to think more deeply, and to become
better informed about policies that can help us create safer and healthier
communities. I hope you’ll join us for this wide-ranging and thought-provoking discussion.
