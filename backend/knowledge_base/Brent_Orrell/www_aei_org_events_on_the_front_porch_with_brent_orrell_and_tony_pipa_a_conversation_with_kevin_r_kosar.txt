Event Summary
On July 22, AEI’s Brent Orrell and Kevin R. Kosar, along with the Brookings Institution’s Tony Pipa, participated in the sixth iteration of the “On the Front Porch” event series. Their discussion centered on Edward C. Banfield’s
Government Project
, which was republished by AEI Press with a new introduction from Dr. Kosar. Mr. Orrell began the event with introductions, then asked Dr. Kosar a series of questions that outlined the main themes in
Government Project
, including why the Casa Grande cooperative failed and what the Casa Grande experiment teaches us about the difficulties of modern community.
Further discussion touched on overcoming Friedrich Hayek’s knowledge problem in federalized, place-based policy and the importance of local identity and the challenges that arise when it fades amid economic decline and development. The event concluded with a Q&A.
—Hunter Dixon
Event Description
Contemporary rural-urban divisions in America are the result of long-term demographic, economic, technological, and social trends. AEI’s Brent Orrell and the Brookings Institution’s Tony Pipa have teamed up to host a series of “On the Front Porch” conversations with authors of recent research on issues facing rural America.
Dr. Orrell and Dr. Pipa will host AEI’s Kevin R. Kosar, who wrote the foreword for a new edition of Edward C. Banfield’s
Government Project
. First published in 1951, Banfield’s book delves into the history and ultimate failure of Casa Grande Valley Farms, a New Deal farming cooperative in Arizona. Dr. Orrell, Dr. Pipa, and Dr. Kosar will explore Banfield’s work on the complexities and challenges of government-sponsored social and economic development programs, especially in the context of rural America.
