	First Step Act: McConnell Delivers for Christmas, and It’s Not a Lump of Coal | American Enterprise Institute - AEI

The House and Senate Judiciary Committees just released the revised text of the First Step Act and it appears criminal justice reform advocates, not to mention federal prisoners and their families, will have something to celebrate this Christmas.
Most of the provisions of the bill remain untouched in this new version.
As I wrote last month
, this is all to the good and is overdue.
The Senate is scheduled to move for final passage of the First Step Act next week, to be followed shortly after by a vote in the full House of Representatives. REUTERS/Mike Theiler
The key sticking point remains provisions in the bill that reform tough sentencing rules from the late 1980s and early 1990s passed in response to high rates of drug-fueled violence. Crime rates have plunged since then but the sentencing rigidities remain, contributing to excessively punitive sentences, over-incarceration, and recidivism. The bill’s authors have made a good-faith effort to address law enforcement concerns by
adjusting “safety-valve” provisions
, modestly narrowing the discretion of judges to take prior criminal history into account in sentencing non-violent offenders.
The new bill also expands the types of offenses that would be ineligible for “earned-time” relief, excluding things like car-jackings that result in serious bodily injury and serious domestic violence. These changes have been enough to bring some long-time opponents of the bill, like Senators David Perdue and Ted Cruz, on board for final passage. Senator Tom Cotton remains against the bill, but he seems increasingly isolated in his opposition.
The Senate is scheduled to move for final passage next week, to be followed shortly after by a vote in the full House of Representatives. With luck, the nation’s federal prisoners and their families will have a new deal for Christmas.
