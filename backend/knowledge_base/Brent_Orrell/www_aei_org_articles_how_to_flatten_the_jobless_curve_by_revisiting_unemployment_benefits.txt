	How to Flatten the Jobless Curve by Revisiting Unemployment Benefits | American Enterprise Institute - AEI

The social distancing and stay at home orders that have begun to flatten the curve of coronavirus infections have imposed great costs on workers. Unemployment that began as a service sector phenomenon is now spreading into every corner of the economy. Even “recession proof” fields like law, medicine, and information technology are seeing business dry up in the vicious downward spiral of recession. Unemployment is projected to reach levels exceeding those of a decade ago and perhaps rising to levels during the Great Depression. Recent news stories showing cars waiting in line for miles to get to food banks demonstrate the seriousness of what people are facing.
Congress and the administration have responded with relief legislation that has boosted and expanded economic support programs, like unemployment insurance, in an effort to help these people cope with job losses. What Congress and the Administration could not do, however, was quickly expand the capacity of the state unemployment insurance systems to manage this vast influx of the newly unemployed. As anyone waiting for hours on hold or seeing their applications repeatedly rejected by overwhelmed online systems can tell you, the antiquated unemployment insurance system, first conceived in the New Deal era, is crumbling under the pressure of processing millions of claims.
