	Americans Face Crisis of Going Back to Work When the Economy Reopens | American Enterprise Institute - AEI

The economy is in
shock. More than 30 million people are now on unemployment with millions more
still waiting to break through online filing systems. Hundreds of thousands
more are being assisted through the federal Paycheck Protection Program.
It was never possible for this induced economic coma to last for long, and states are already taking tentative steps toward reopening their economies. Some, like Georgia, Texas, and South Carolina, are moving sooner and faster than others. Many workers will be able to pick up where they left off, but millions of others are likely to find their old jobs gone forever. Regional labor market conditions will vary widely across the country depending upon the relative dominance of economic sectors in a given metropolitan area. Places like Las Vegas and Central Florida, which rely heavily on tourism and hospitality, will have very different challenges than states where the information economy or manufacturing are dominant.
