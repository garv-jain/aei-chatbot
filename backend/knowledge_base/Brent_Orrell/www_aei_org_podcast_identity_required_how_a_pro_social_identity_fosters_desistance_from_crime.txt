	Identity Required: How a Pro-social Identity Fosters Desistance from Crime | American Enterprise Institute - AEI

Desistance, the decision to abandon criminal behavior, is one of the great puzzles in criminal justice policy. Shawn D. Bushway of the RAND Corporation argues that desistance depends on achieving a new, noncriminal, pro-social identity, which is often a long and arduous process supported by empirical evidence.
This rebroadcast of our December 14 event contains a discussion moderated by Brent on Bushway’s
report
“Reentry, desistance, and identity achievement.” The event features remarks from Bushway, followed by comments from Rashawn Ray of the Brookings Institution, Bret Bucklen of the Pennsylvania Department of Corrections, and Julio Medina of Exodus Transitional Community.
