	Rethinking Reentry | American Enterprise Institute - AEI

Criminal justice reform has been a bipartisan issue in recent years, with Congress and state legislatures taking important steps to reduce the prison population and ensure those in the justice system are given the opportunity and support needed for effective reintegration. At the same time, we still have a long way to go. Our recent efforts at rehabilitation have fallen far short of success, with over 2/3 of the nearly 600,000 people exiting prison every year in the US arrested again within 3 years of their release.
AEI’s new volume, “
Rethinking Reentry
,” takes a fresh look at this issue, featuring the work of several of the leading voices from the last 30 years of reentry research to discuss what has worked, what hasn’t, and what can be done to improve our efforts – and the future of returning citizens – moving forward. Complete with a foreword by former Speaker of the House Paul Ryan, the volume provides a needed framework for evidence-based reentry programming that will enable more people to leave the revolving door of the justice system behind for good.
In addition to
the volume
, this page includes a myriad of resources related to AEI’s work on criminal justice reform and reentry, including podcasts, blogs, op-eds, and videos from leading scholars around the country. Whether you are a researcher, policymaker, practitioner, or concerned citizen, we hope you will find this work helpful and will continue to engage with us as we chart a path to a safer and more prosperous future for returning citizens and their communities.
